A logistics company is upgrading its fleet management system to support predictive maintenance. The team has identified several backlog items that no longer align with the revised system requirements and delivery goals. The project manager has analyzed recent performance gaps and facilitated cross-functional input from the operations and IT departments. What should the project manager do next?

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

A. Request a scope change to remove outdated work items and match new needs. Scope change initiation is the appropriate next step when work items no longer align with updated requirements. This ensures traceability, delivery integrity, and stakeholder transparency. The other options are incorrect. Reordering delivery before initiating scope change is premature and risks misalignment and wasted effort. The project manager should perform scope validation before sequencing adjustments. Extending the timeline before initiating a scope change disrupts planning integrity. Scope validation should occur before delivery parameters are adjusted. Updating the stakeholder register is out of order and needs to happen as the first step in this process, instead of in this step.
