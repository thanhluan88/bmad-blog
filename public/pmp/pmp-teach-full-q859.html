Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

C. Coach stakeholders on agile principles and help them understand the value of attending iteration review meetings. The project manager should coach stakeholders on the benefits of regular iteration reviews, which enable collaboration, real-time feedback, and adjustments. Educating stakeholders on agile principles and gaining their buy-in ensures effective implementation. Iteration reviews offer valuable, timely feedback that supports collaboration and adaptability, both central to agile success. The other options are incorrect. Allowing stakeholders to choose the communication method does not guarantee that the communication strategy follows agile principles and misses the opportunity to coach and educate stakeholders. If the communication strategy must be tailored, it is important to ensure they understand the impact and offer them tailored reporting options while maintaining the agile process. Monthly reporting is not aligned with agile principles, which emphasize flexibility, adaptability, and continuous feedback. Monthly reporting can delay the feedback loop, potentially causing issues to be identified too late. Regular iteration reviews keep stakeholders engaged and involved. Dismissing participation misses the need to educate about agile. Dashboards are useful sources of information, but relying only on them undermines agile collaboration.
