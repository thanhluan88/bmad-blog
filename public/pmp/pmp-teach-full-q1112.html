A project manager is leading a complex software integration project involving legacy systems and emerging technologies. During the initial risk identification process, the internal team identified standard technical and schedule risks. However, stakeholders are concerned about potential integration challenges and regulatory compliance issues that the team may not fully understand. The project has tight deadlines and a limited budget. What should the project manager do to enhance the risk identification process?

Đáp án

A. Engage external subject matter experts to validate identified risks and uncover additional vulnerabilities.

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

A. Engage external subject matter experts to validate identified risks and uncover additional vulnerabilities. Subject matter experts have specialized expertise and can help identify and address high-risk issues. Subject matter experts are invited to the meeting because they provide functional expertise that will help identify high-risk areas quickly. Expert judgment aids in risk identification by applying knowledge in a specific subject, industry segment, organizational processes, etc. The other options are incorrect. Repeated brainstorming without structure can lead to scope creep in the risk register and hinder decision-making. Historical data is useful as part of a broader strategy, but on its own, it can overlook the unique aspects of the current integration, leading to incomplete or outdated risk profiles. One-on-one interviews are time-consuming and may cause delays in progressing to risk analysis and mitigation.
