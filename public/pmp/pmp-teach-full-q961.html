Chi tiết tiếng Anh — Why this answer

1. Đáp án tham chiếu (Reference solution)

  • C. Examine the organizational process assets (OPAs) to define the structure, rules, procedures, and policies the project must follow.
  • OPAs include any artifact, practice, or knowledge from the performing organizations involved in the project, including but not limited to policies, processes, procedures, and organizational knowledge repositories.

2. Lý luận PMBOK 8 (PMBOK reasoning)

  • Scenario: A major manufacturing corporation is launching a lucrative new initiative that must meet strict internal and external regulations and policies.
  • PMBOK 8, p. 21 (Manage Project Execution): 4 Manage Project Execution The Manage Project Execution process involves leading and performing the work defined in the project management plan and implementing approved changes… — which is why this PMBOK guidance applies directly to the stem and supports the correct PM action.
  • Therefore C is correct: OPAs include any artifact, practice, or knowledge from the performing organizations involved in the project, including but not limited to policies, processes, procedures, and organizational knowledge repositories.

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

C. Examine the organizational process assets (OPAs) to define the structure, rules, procedures, and policies the project must follow. OPAs include any artifact, practice, or knowledge from the performing organizations involved in the project, including but not limited to policies, processes, procedures, and organizational knowledge repositories. OPAs are inputs to many of the project management processes and guide and influence the management of the project. The other options are incorrect: The project charter is specific to the project and does not include all relevant organizational policies and resources.The creation of a project charter is vital to the functionality of the project team and the defining of the project manager’s authority, but it does not inherently provide the project manager with the relevant data and information. The project manager must use the authority granted to them by the project charter to access and leverage the organizational process assets and provide their team with the necessary information. The project team may utilize the OPAs when creating the resource management plan to ensure that all selected resources are in line with internal and external policies and adhere to the findings of the data collected. The resource management plan does not inherently guarantee effective governance on its own. Work performance data from past and current projects may be included in the OPAs, but it must be analyzed alongside other artifacts and data to understand the whole scope of the project’s management.
