Contract terms include penalty clauses, but enforcement could strain the vendor relationship needed for future phases. What should the project manager do first to address this situation?

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

A. Conduct a comprehensive impact analysis of noncompliance with project objectives and quality standards. Before taking any corrective action, the project manager must understand the full impact of the noncompliance on project objectives, schedule, cost, and quality. This analysis provides the foundation for making informed decisions about remediation approaches, vendor relationships, and stakeholder communications. The other options are incorrect. Reviewing contract specifications only focuses on accountability without understanding the actual project impact. Taking legal action at this point is premature without evaluating whether the impact justifies such measures. Seeking alternative vendors may be necessary, but the immediate focus should be on assessing the impact. There are limited alternatives available, and it is unknown if finding a new vendor is necessary. Clarifying the specifications may be sufficient to prevent similar issues in the future with the current or any alternative vendor.
