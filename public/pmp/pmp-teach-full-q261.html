1. Câu hỏi thực hành — Full Bank Q261

pmp-full-questions · Câu 261
A project manager is working on a high-visibility initiative with multiple sponsors and stakeholders. One of the key sponsors required a monthly progress report and appointed a liaison to coordinate updates. Weeks into the project, the liaison begins requesting detailed weekly reports. The project manager complies, assuming the sponsor is kept informed. However, during a steering committee meeting, the sponsor criticizes the lack of monthly updates and claims they are unaware of project progress. What should the project manager do to address this situation and avoid similar issues in the future?

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

B. Facilitate a joint discussion with the sponsor and the liaison to clarify expectations and reporting needs. The project manager should bring both the sponsor and liaison together to clarify expectations, establish reporting formats and frequency, and ensure everyone is aligned going forward. This approach prevents future misunderstandings and reinforces stakeholder trust. The other options are incorrect. Summarizing and forwarding past reports may seem helpful, but it does not address the root cause — a misalignment in communication channels and expectations. Escalating to the PMO without first trying to resolve the issue collaboratively shows poor stakeholder management and might damage trust. Formalizing reporting requests could help later, but it doesn’t resolve the confusion that already occurred. Alignment must be established first through direct engagement.
