A project manager overseeing a construction project discovers that a required government permit has not yet been secured. Obtaining the permit will delay the project by 21 days and incur a cost of US$40,000. However, proceeding without the permit would result in a fine of US$1,900 per day. What should the project manager do next?

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

A. Apply for the permit, accept the 21-day delay and US$40,000 cost, and update the schedule and cost baselines accordingly. Obtaining the required permit is crucial for complying with local regulations and avoiding potential legal issues and fines. While it will delay the project, it is the responsible and ethical course of action as it ensures legal compliance and minimizes the risk of costly fines in the long run. The other options are incorrect. Proceeding without obtaining a permit is both irresponsible and unethical for a project manager. The organization risks facing severe penalties, legal consequences, and potential project shutdown. Attempting to negotiate with the local government is generally not advisable. Negotiations are unlikely to succeed, have ethical implications, and could cause further delays. Allocating the fine amount as an additional project expense suggests budgeting for fines, which is neither ethical nor cost-effective. The fines could accumulate to a substantial sum, and the project manager would still face the risk of being fined and having the project shut down.
