Stakeholder interviews during project planning reveal conflicting expectations. Operations management wants rapid deployment to reduce costs, but compliance management demands extensive documentation to meet audit requirements. These positions will affect both scope and schedule if this misalignment is not resolved appropriately before execution. What should the project manager do?

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

A. Facilitate a joint session to reconcile misaligned expectations. Facilitating a joint session to reconcile expectations is correct because collaborative facilitation surfaces interests, explores trade‑offs, and enables documented alignment early, reducing downstream rework. The other options are incorrect. Documenting both expectations and proceeding with the majority view is incorrect because it does not guarantee buy-in. Choosing an expectation that aligns with the sponsor’s view is incorrect because it could be biased and risks undermining other stakeholders’ commitment and support. Delaying the decision until execution is incorrect because deferring misalignment increases risk, resentment, and last-minute rework.
