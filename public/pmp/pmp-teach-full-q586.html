1. Câu hỏi thực hành — Full Bank Q586

pmp-full-questions · Câu 586
A pharmaceutical company has assembled a project team to research and develop a groundbreaking new medication. Given the complexity and uncertainty of the development process, the project’s timeline and costs are likely to fluctuate significantly. As the project manager, you are responsible for selecting and implementing a contract before beginning the work. What type of contract would provide the necessary flexibility while minimizing financial risk for the client?

Answer

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

C. A cost-reimbursable contract. A cost-reimbursable contract is well-suited for complex and uncertain projects because it allows for flexibility in adjusting to the changing needs and scope of the development effort. The client bears the actual costs incurred by the contractor, plus an agreed fee, which reduces the contractor's financial risk and encourages thorough exploration without fixed constraints. This approach also mitigates financial risk for the contractor and ensures the client only pays for actual work performed. The other options are incorrect. While a T&M contract provides flexibility and is commonly used when scope is not fully determined, it does not effectively manage financial risk for the client. A fixed-price contract would not be appropriate in this scenario due to the project's inherent uncertainty. It would place an undue burden on the contractor to complete the project within a defined budget, leading to compromised quality or incomplete work if unforeseen challenges arise. A target-cost contract is typically used to motivate efficiency by sharing cost savings or overruns between the client and contractor. However, in this case, where the development timeline and costs are highly uncertain, this type of contract is less suitable.
