During a project planning session for a complex product, the project manager is defining a release plan to ensure continuous value delivery and maintain customer engagement. A senior stakeholder proposes delaying all releases until project completion so the customer can experience all features at once. What should the project manager do?

Đáp án

D. Deliver the product over several short iterations to gather customer feedback and refine features during development.

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

D. Deliver the product over several short iterations to gather customer feedback and refine features during development. Delivering the product over several short iterations enables frequent customer feedback, allowing the team to refine features continuously. This approach increases alignment with stakeholder needs, reduces the risk of late rework, and supports adaptive planning. The other options are incorrect. While collaborating on a balanced release schedule is beneficial, it does not guarantee early and frequent feedback from the customer throughout development. Phased releases at major milestones still delay feedback until later stages, which can increase the risk of misalignment with customer expectations. Targeted releases for completed features help with quality control, but they do not necessarily ensure iterative customer engagement and adaptation across the entire product.
