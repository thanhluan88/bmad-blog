Practice Questions — PMBOK 8th Ed

Bài học Q76 về Communications Management Plan — Nadia tại CH Systems, hospital expansion: stakeholders phàn nàn không được involve, business units không engaged cho release gần nhất → dissatisfied users.

PMBOK Guide 8, p.72–73 & 117–118 Plan Communications Management Communications Management Plan Câu 76 — Practice

1. Plan Communications Management (p.72–73)

Process defines how to communicate with stakeholders and aligns with stakeholder engagement for consistency. The communications management plan (p.117–118) describes how, when, and by whom information will be administered and disseminated.

How Format, channel, method
When Frequency, timing
By whom Owner, disseminator

Instructor: Not involved / not engaged

Stem signals communication breakdown — parts of business missed the release. Next step: create communications management plan (D) to structure how stakeholders stay informed and involved.

2. So sánh các đáp án

OptionActionQ76 fit?
A. Identify stakeholders Find who can be more involved Stakeholders already complaining — identification done
B. Stakeholder register Document stakeholder info Q73 artifact — doesn't fix comms/involvement gap
C. Engagement activities Add/update engagement actions Execution — comms plan defines how info reaches them first
D. Comms management plan How/when/by whom to communicate Yes — aligns engagement + communication

Engagement vs Communications

Q75 engagement matrix maps gaps. Q76: stakeholders not informed/involved in release → plan how communication happens (comms management plan).

3. Câu hỏi thực hành — Question 76

Practice Questions PMBOK 8th Ed · Q76
Nadia is initiating the next phase of a hospital expansion project at CH Systems. Early in planning, Nadia receives complaints from some stakeholders that they are not being involved. Some parts of the business were not engaged for the most recent release, resulting in dissatisfied users. What will Nadia do next?

4. Phân tích đáp án — Đáp án đúng: D

Tại sao chọn D?

  • Not involved + not engaged for release = communication/involvement breakdown.
  • Comms plan defines how, when, by whom info is shared (p.117–118).
  • Aligns with stakeholder engagement for consistency (p.72–73).

Loại trừ từng đáp án

Đáp ánTại sao không đúng
A. Identify stakeholders They're already voicing complaints — known but not properly communicated with.
B. Stakeholder register Documents who they are — doesn't establish how/when they receive information.
C. Engagement activities Tactical actions — PMI wants comms plan first to structure dissemination.

5. Drill — Artifact hay action?

Define how, when, and by whom status updates reach business units
New vendor stakeholder appears — document contact and influence
Schedule one-on-one meetings with resistant department heads
Discover community group affected by construction noise

0 / 4 đúng

6. Bẫy thi

Bẫy 1: Not involved → identify (A)

Stakeholders are complaining — they're identified. Problem is communication structure, not discovery.

Bẫy 2: Stakeholder → register (B)

Q73 pattern doesn't apply — stem is about missed involvement in release, not new stakeholder.

Bẫy 3: Engaged → engagement activities (C)

Activities execute engagement — comms plan defines how information reaches stakeholders consistently.

Pattern

Not involved · not engaged · dissatisfied users · missed release → communications management plan (D).
