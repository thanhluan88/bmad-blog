Midway through execution, the project team encounters multiple unexpected issues that directly threaten both schedule and budget performance. Many of these issues are on the critical path and must be resolved immediately before dependent work can proceed. According to the approved communications management plan, the client and key stakeholders receive formal updates only once a month. Several of the current issues require decisions that could significantly impact contractual commitments and project risk exposure. What should the project manager do?

Đáp án

C. Increase the frequency and detail of communications to stakeholders to support timely decisions and management.

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

C. Increase the frequency and detail of communications to stakeholders to support timely decisions and management. The project manager must tailor communication to meet the needs of the situation. When urgent issues require stakeholder decisions, the project manager should adapt the frequency and content of communication immediately, even if the communications plan specifies a longer interval. This ensures decisions are made in time to protect the schedule, budget, and contractual obligations. All other options are incorrect. Documenting issues internally does not involve stakeholders in critical decision-making, increasing the risk of delays or cost overruns. Strictly following the monthly cadence ignores the urgency of the current situation and may result in missed opportunities for timely intervention. While updating the communications plan can be valuable for long-term alignment, seeking formal approval before addressing urgent issues can waste critical time and worsen project impact.
