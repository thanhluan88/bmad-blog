Why this answer

1. Reference solution

  • D. Conduct a confidential team satisfaction evaluation to uncover root causes affecting morale and performance.
  • Before implementing corrective measures, the project manager should identify the underlying causes of low morale and inefficiency.

2. PMBOK 8 reasoning

  • Scenario: During a mid-project review in a hybrid delivery environment, regular check-ins with the cross-functional delivery team reveal a drop in morale, declining productivity, and an increase in missed task deadlines.
  • PMBOK 8, p. 53 (Performance Domain: Resources, Develop Team): 8 Build an Empowered Culture To develop an empowered project culture, the project environment should promote mutual trust among stakeholders and the project team members. — so the PM must address the relationship directly with transparency before escalating or assigning blame.
  • Therefore D is correct: Before implementing corrective measures, the project manager should identify the underlying causes of low morale and inefficiency.

Answer

D. Conduct a confidential team satisfaction evaluation to uncover root causes affecting morale and performance.

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

D. Conduct a confidential team satisfaction evaluation to uncover root causes affecting morale and performance. Before implementing corrective measures, the project manager should identify the underlying causes of low morale and inefficiency. A satisfaction and engagement evaluation provides actionable insights, enabling targeted interventions rather than assumptions. All other options are incorrect. Skill assessments focus on capability gaps, which may not address morale or motivation issues. Team-building can help with cohesion, but without understanding root causes, it risks being a superficial fix. Removing roadblocks addresses workflow efficiency but does not necessarily improve morale if other causes are present.
