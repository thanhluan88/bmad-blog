1. Câu hỏi thực hành — Full Bank Q689

pmp-full-questions · Câu 689
A critical project component is available from only one supplier. Midway through the project, the supplier doubles the price of the component, citing a market shortage. The project sponsor pressures the project manager to accept the new pricing quickly to avoid disruption to the supply. The finance manager refuses, saying the increase will push the budget beyond its limits. The procurement manager warns that alternate suppliers are hard to find and must be verified before purchases can be approved. What should the project manager do?

Answer

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

A. Meet with the sponsor, finance, and procurement managers to negotiate terms while evaluating alternative sources. The sponsor, finance manager, and procurement manager all have competing priorities in this scenario. The sponsor is focused on schedule and continuity, the finance manager on budget constraints, and the procurement manager on supplier verification. The project manager’s role is to engage all relevant stakeholders to collaboratively find a viable solution that minimizes impact on the project. Assessing alternative sources while negotiating allows the project team to maintain flexibility and avoid being entirely dependent on the current supplier or locked into unfavorable terms. The other options are incorrect. Approving the price increase prematurely without proper evaluation of alternatives could result in financial consequences for the project and reduce stakeholder trust. Canceling the agreement without securing a verified replacement supplier introduces significant risks to the project. Alternate suppliers are difficult to find and must be verified, adding time and complexity to this approach. Redesigning a critical component requires time, testing, and validation, which could significantly delay the project. This option alone does not address the immediate budget, procurement, and schedule pressures.
