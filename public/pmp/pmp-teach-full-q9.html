Đáp án

C. Explain that agile teams must maintain governance and WIP limits, and begin work on the landing page only when capacity is available, or when other priorities change.

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

C. Explain that agile teams must follow governance and WIP limits and only start work on the landing page when there is capacity or if another item is deprioritized. This option reinforces that agile approaches still rely on governance mechanisms, including WIP limits, explicit policies, and collaborative backlog prioritization. The proper response is to explain that the team cannot bypass agreed ways of working simply because the request is urgent or from a senior stakeholder. The product owner and stakeholders should adjust priorities and sequencing within the governance framework. Only after reprioritization and available capacity is the new item pulled into “In Progress.” The other options are incorrect. Adding the “Priority Campaign Landing Page” directly into “In Progress” bypasses the explicitly agreed WIP limit and governance. Sacrificing process discipline to please a stakeholder creates systemic risk: multitasking, reduced flow efficiency, quality issues, and an expectation that rules can be ignored. Adding the item into “To Do,” and moving the lowest-priority “In Progress” item back to “To Do” respects WIP limits. However, it is still incorrect because it unilaterally reshuffles work without transparent collaboration or product owner input. Quietly moving items to make space avoids conflict but undermines alignment, stakeholder expectations, and agile transparency. Relying on overtime to sidestep governance and WIP limits is not sustainable, may harm team morale, and signals that rules and capacity constraints can be ignored if pressure is high enough. It does not address the core need for prioritization and adherence to the agreed process.
