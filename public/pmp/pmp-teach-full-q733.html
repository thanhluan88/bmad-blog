1. Câu hỏi thực hành — Full Bank Q733

pmp-full-questions · Câu 733
A project manager is finishing a project in a complex environment where the initial scope, timeline, and resources have evolved over time. The sponsor is requesting insights on the realization of benefits and the overall value delivered by the project. The project manager needs to calculate the value of the project outcomes, considering both the planned benefits and any additional value that may have emerged throughout the project life cycle. The project manager also needs to consider any challenges faced during execution, such as budget deviations, changes in scope, and varying stakeholder expectations. How should the project manager calculate the value of the project outcomes?

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

A. Evaluate both qualitative and quantitative project benefits, considering their alignment with the project's strategic goals. Calculating the value of project outcomes requires an understanding of both qualitative and quantitative benefits. This approach ensures a comprehensive view, considering strategic alignment, stakeholder expectations, and measurable impacts throughout the project life cycle. The other options are incorrect. Calculating ROI by dividing tangible benefits by expenses oversimplifies the process and ignores important strategic and non-financial benefits that are crucial for a complete benefits realization assessment. Performing a retrospective financial assessment may provide insights into financial performance, but it does not capture the full scope of project outcomes, including non-financial and strategic benefits. Comparing outcomes only with the initial budget focuses too narrowly on financial metrics, failing to consider other dimensions of project success, such as stakeholder satisfaction and alignment with business objectives.
