A pharmaceutical company is developing an experimental product. Several stakeholders warn that the project might not achieve its intended objectives. The sponsor urges the project manager to take proactive measures and prepare for the possibility of project termination before the scheduled completion date. What should the project manager do to prepare for the possibility of project termination before the scheduled completion date? (Choose 2)

Đáp án

A,E

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

A, E A. Define the exit criteria and thresholds with the sponsor and key stakeholders. E. Prepare an early-closure plan that includes project knowledge transfer. Given that this is an experimental product, defining exit criteria and thresholds and preparing an early-closure plan are proactive actions. It sets decision rules for early termination and is exactly what the sponsor requested. Preparing an early-closure plan is a concrete execution measure: contracts, obligations, and knowledge transfer. It is key to preparedness. The other options are less effective. Updating the risk management plan in response to new threats as they materialize is important, but it is reactive. This option is not a proactive preparation step and does not align with the sponsor’s request to prepare for the possibility of project termination. Contingency reserves are designed to address specific risks (known-unknowns) and should be based on an understanding of their probability and potential impact. Allocating a broad reserve indicates insufficient risk analysis and does not allow for tailored responses to distinct risk scenarios. Increasing reporting frequency but postponing action is misaligned with the sponsor's directive. Monitoring without action is reactive, not proactive.
