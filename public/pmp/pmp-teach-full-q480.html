A global retailer is upgrading its inventory management system. Midway through the project, the finance partner tells the project manager that travel and contractor costs are running 7% below plan. The sponsor expects an update at a steering committee meeting next week. What should the project manager do first?

Answer

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

D. Analyze cost variances and update the forecast before briefing the sponsor. Even though costs are 7% below plan, the project manager still needs to understand why. Underspending can mean work is delayed, activities weren’t performed as planned, or estimates were inaccurate. The project manager should analyze the variance, update the cost forecast (EAC/ETC), and be prepared to explain whether this underrun is a genuine efficiency gain, a timing difference, or a risk to scope/schedule. Then they can brief the sponsor and steering committee with a clear, data-driven update and implications for the rest of the project. The other options are incorrect. There is no indication that the project is short of funds. Asking for more budget without analysis or need would be unjustified. Underspending is not a reason to freeze discretionary spending. Some discretionary activities (training, stakeholder engagement, extra validation) can be important to achieving quality outcomes. Altering the classification of expenses to conceal a variance, whether positive or negative, results in a misrepresentation of the project's financial position.
