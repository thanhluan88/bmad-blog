A regional bank is launching a mobile app upgrade to improve customer self-service. While preparing to approve the integrated baseline, the project manager notices that the scope assumes in-house security testing, but the schedule and budget assume using an external testing vendor. The sponsor is asking for the baseline by tomorrow. What should the project manager do?

Solution gốc (CSV — cột P)

B. Work with stakeholders to reconcile scope, schedule, and cost assumptions before baselining. A baseline should reflect a realistic, internally consistent plan. Here, there is a clear inconsistency indicating that the integrated baseline (scope, schedule, and cost together) is not aligned. Before approving a baseline, the project manager should bring the relevant stakeholders together to clarify which approach will be used and then adjust scope, schedule, and cost so they match. Only after this reconciliation should the baseline be set; otherwise, performance against the baseline will be misleading and difficult to control. The other options are incorrect. Issues identified before baselining should be resolved before the baseline is approved, not deferred into execution. If scope, schedule, and budget are misaligned from the start, decision-making will be based on an unrealistic plan. The project manager’s role is to facilitate alignment across scope, schedule, and cost, not to push an ill-defined choice to the sponsor without first reconciling the assumptions and implications with stakeholders. Removing security testing to make the numbers fit jeopardizes the project’s success and changes the project’s objectives without due consideration or formal change control.
