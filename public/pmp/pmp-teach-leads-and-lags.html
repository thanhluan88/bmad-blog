Practice Questions — PMBOK 8th Ed

Bài học Q60 về Leads and Lags — Reina, AC Goods product launch: labeling bắt đầu khi drafts approved (trước khi packaging hoàn tất); quality inspection wait two days sau production. PM nên làm gì?

PMBOK Guide 8, p.50 & 179–180 Develop Schedule Leads and Lags Câu 60 — Practice

1. Leads and lags (p.179–180)

Leads and lags adjust timing of successor activities relative to predecessors within dependency relationships — cho phép start sớm hơn (lead) hoặc delay có chủ đích (lag).

TermMeaningExample
Lead Successor starts before predecessor finishes Labeling starts when drafts approved (packaging still in progress)
Lag Intentional delay after predecessor finishes QI waits 2 days after production finishes

Develop Schedule (p.50)

Leads/lags are applied when building the schedule model — define dependency timing, not change durations (D) or compress schedule (A/C).

2. Map scenario → lead & lag

FS + lead vs FS + lag

Finish-to-Start with lead = overlap (negative lag in some tools). FS with lag = gap between finish and start (e.g. 2-day wait).

3. Câu hỏi thực hành — Question 60

Practice Questions PMBOK 8th Ed · Q60
Reina is managing a new product launch at AC Goods. Packaging design must be finalized before mass production, but labeling can begin once initial drafts are approved. Additionally, quality inspection must wait two days after production finishes. What should Reina do next?

4. Phân tích đáp án — Đáp án đúng: B

Tại sao chọn B?

  • Drafts approved → labeling early start = lead.
  • Wait 2 days after production = lag on dependency.
  • Both are dependency timing adjustments in Develop Schedule (p.50, 179–180).

Loại trừ từng đáp án

Đáp ánTại sao không đúng
A. Fast tracking Overlap phases to shorten schedule — không define specific lead/lag on dependencies.
C. Schedule crashing Add resources/cost to shorten — không về timing between linked activities.
D. Update durations Change how long activities take — stem cần adjust when successors start.

Lead vs fast tracking

Fast tracking = compression technique (risk). Lead/lag = fine-tune dependency relationships trong schedule model — đúng level cho Q60.

5. Drill — Lead, lag, fast-track, crash hay duration?

Successor starts when drafts approved, before predecessor done
Wait 2 days after production before inspection
Run design and build in parallel to shorten project
Activity takes 5 days instead of 7 — revise estimate

0 / 4 đúng

6. Bẫy thi

Bẫy 1: Overlap → Fast tracking (A)

Overlap có thể liên quan — nhưng stem mô tả cụ thể lead (drafts) và lag (2 days) → leads & lags (B).

Bẫy 2: Shorten → Crashing (C)

Crashing = add resources. Không phải adjust dependency timing.

Bẫy 3: Schedule → Update durations (D)

Durations = how long. Lead/lag = when successor starts relative to predecessor.

Pattern

Begin early when X approved · wait N days after Y finishes → Apply leads and lags (B).
